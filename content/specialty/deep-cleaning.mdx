## When You Actually Need Deep Cleaning

Deep cleaning isn't routine. It's strategic. You need it at specific moments when your facility requires a complete operational reset.

**Post-Construction or Major Renovation**
After construction wraps, you're left with dust everywhere. Equipment areas are filthy. Windows are hazed. The facility isn't move-in ready until someone does a complete deep clean.
We remove construction dust from every surface, clean equipment areas so your machinery runs properly, detail every vent and light fixture, and prepare your space for occupancy. Move-in happens on schedule, not delayed by cleaning.

**Seasonal or Annual Reset**
Over a year, dust accumulates in unreached spaces. Equipment areas build up debris. Corners and baseboards get overlooked. A seasonal deep clean brings your facility back to baseline.
Most facilities do this quarterly or annually. It prevents operational decline and maintains compliance standards.

**Before Inspections or Audits**
Inspectors look for detail. Dirty equipment areas, dusty surfaces, neglected corners—these raise red flags, even if your daily cleaning is fine.
A pre-audit deep clean ensures your facility passes inspection. Every detail matters. We handle them.

**Move-In or Move-Out Preparation**
Empty facilities need to be pristine before new tenants arrive. We clean everything—every room, every surface, every vent. Your facility makes a professional impression.

**After Prolonged Vacancy**
Extended vacancy leaves facilities in rough shape. Dust settles everywhere. Equipment areas accumulate debris. Restrooms need deep sanitization.
A thorough deep clean brings your facility back to operational standard, ready for occupancy or tenant transition.

**Before Major Events or Client Visits**
When high-stakes clients or inspectors are coming, your facility needs to shine. Deep cleaning ensures every detail is perfect.

**Safety and Compliance Recovery**
Sometimes facilities fall behind on maintenance. Equipment areas become safety risks. Surfaces accumulate contamination. A deep clean resets everything and brings you back into compliance.

## The Deep Cleaning Difference

Deep cleaning isn't just "more thorough." It's a different approach entirely.

**Routine cleaning** handles surfaces people see daily. Floors, restrooms, common areas. It's ongoing, preventative.

**Deep cleaning** reaches everywhere else. High surfaces, equipment areas, hidden corners, light fixtures, vents, baseboards. It's comprehensive and occasional—triggered by specific events.

Most facilities confuse the two. They assume daily cleaning should handle everything. It can't. Daily crews manage high-traffic areas. Deep cleaning resets the entire facility.

We treat deep cleaning as a separate operation:

- Specialized equipment (pressure washers, extraction systems, access equipment)
- Trained crews trained in restoration techniques
- Systematic approach (nothing gets missed)
- Documentation (photos showing before/after, completion of every area)

**That's the difference between clean and actually clean.**

## Our Deep Cleaning Process

**Step 1: Complete Facility Assessment**
We start by understanding your facility's scope, condition, and specific needs. We evaluate facility size and layout, surface types, current condition, access challenges, and timelines. The output is a detailed scope document and timeline so you know exactly what's happening and when.

**Step 2: Area Preparation and Furniture Moving**
Before we clean, we prepare the space. All moveable furniture is carefully moved to clear floor space, equipment areas are prepped for access, protective coverings placed where needed, and work zones established to minimize disruption. Client approval before any deep work begins.

**Step 3: Top-to-Bottom Systematic Cleaning**
This is where deep cleaning actually happens. High surfaces and ceilings, walls and trim, equipment areas, floors and surfaces, restrooms and kitchens, windows and glass - all systematically detailed.

**Step 4: Specialized Surface Treatments**
Different surfaces need different approaches. We apply treatments matched to your facility's specific needs, including professional carpet extraction, hard floor treatment (VCT stripping/waxing, concrete sealing/polishing, hardwood restoration), equipment area degreasing/restoration, and wall/trim paint touch-ups.

**Step 5: Final Inspection and Touch-Ups**
Before we're done, we inspect everything. Every detail is verified against our comprehensive checklist. Any missed spots are corrected immediately, final details refined, quality verification complete, followed by a final client walkthrough and approval.

**Step 6: Documentation and Handoff**
You receive complete documentation of what was done, including before and after photos showing the transformation, a detailed completion report spanning all areas covered and treatments applied, a timeline of work performed, recommendations for ongoing maintenance, and warranty on work performed (if applicable).
